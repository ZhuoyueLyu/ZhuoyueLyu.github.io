Topic: Relationship between deterioration rate and identifying accuracy.


Phase 1 (learning/trial & error):

The major work of our team is to develop a standard and efficient way to test and evaluate the ADAS system on a simulation platform, our team has plentiful datasets with good quality, which helps my project a lot.

After spending the first two weeks learning the basic concepts of Machine Learning, using sources like Google Crash Course and ML course by Andrew Ng, I create my first decision tree model.
(I was really excited at that time, since I know nothing about ML before, but now I creat something \(≧▽≦)/ ! )

Then I found that Google Inception V3 can be retrained using TensorFlow, follow the guidance I fed 5 categories, 3000+ in total of flower pictures and train the model with 500 steps:

The model accurately recognize a dandelion picture (testing set) with a score of 98.9%:



Then I start to add different noises (Salt&Pepper, Gaussian) to the image:




the result (based on the last image) was okay, but we can see the decreasing of score.

Phase 2 (Real challenge):

On the third week, I use CARLA to generate 11, 600 images of the different street signs under different weather, lighting, blocking conditions:



I randomly pick some of the images to create a testing set:



and feed the other images (training set) to the inception V3 and train it for 500 steps, After getting a model with precision over 85%, I decide to change the size of the image in the testing set and test again (this result was based on a "weight4t" image):



I started to wonder how to model the relationship between accuracy and deteration rate, at first, I think it might depends on the information entropy, but after calculated several times, I realized that it's not true, since we can have a picture with the same information entropy but without any street sign inside at all.

Having no idea what to do, I turned to Dr. Xue for help, and he suggested me to read this paper, since they've created very robust adversarial examples that can "cheat" the classifier!



he said that maybe there is no easy or clear relationship between accuracy and image, and there are other people working on that too, but no luck so far.

Although I didn’t find the relationship, I did learn a lot (ML, TensorFlow, reading papers, teamwork etc.) along the way, which is still pretty meaningful and interesting, and I’ve enrolled in a Computer Vision course next semester, I believe I will have a better understanding by then.
